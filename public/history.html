The History of Coffee

From Ethiopian forests to modern cafés around the world

A Journey Through Time

9th Century — Ethiopian Discovery

Legend says a goat herder named Kaldi discovered coffee when his goats became energetic after eating berries from a mysterious plant. This discovery eventually led monks to brew the berries into a drink that helped them stay awake during prayers.

15th Century — Arabian Coffee Houses

Coffee cultivation began in Yemen. Soon coffee houses called “Qahveh Khaneh” appeared across the Middle East where people gathered to drink coffee, listen to music, and discuss ideas.

17th Century — Coffee Reaches Europe

Coffee arrived in Europe through trade routes. Coffeehouses quickly became intellectual hubs where artists, writers, and philosophers gathered.

20th Century — The Rise of Modern Cafés

With innovations like espresso machines and global coffee chains, coffee became a cultural experience enjoyed worldwide.

Interesting Coffee Facts

☕ Coffee Beans

🌍 Global Drink

⚡ Energy Boost

Caffeine stimulates the brain and improves focus.

📅 Daily Cups

Over 2 billion cups of coffee are consumed daily worldwide.

How Coffee Evolved

From ancient Ethiopian forests to sophisticated modern cafés, coffee has transformed from a simple beverage into a global culture.

Today, coffee innovation includes specialty brewing techniques, artisan roasting, sustainable sourcing, and unique flavor experiments.

Coffee is no longer just a drink — it is a community, a lifestyle, and a symbol of creativity and conversation.
